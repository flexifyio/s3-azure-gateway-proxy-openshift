## Responsibilities

### All Maintainers

- Review pull requests in a timely manner
- Maintain code quality and testing standards
- Monitor and respond to issues
- Guide project direction and strategy
- Uphold Code of Conduct
- Participate in security patch coordination
- Support the community

## Maintainer Onboarding

As the project grows, we may invite new maintainers from the contributor community.

**Criteria for consideration:**
- Sustained, high-quality contributions
- Understanding of project goals and architecture
- Community respect and positive interactions
- Time commitment to maintain responsibilities
- Alignment with project values

**Onboarding process:**
1. Private discussion with existing maintainers
2. Invitation and discussion with candidate
3. Probationary period (30-60 days)
4. Full maintainer status upon success

## Emeritus Maintainers

When a maintainer needs to step down, they may be recognized as an emeritus maintainer.

**Emeritus status includes:**
- Recognition for past contributions
- Optional continued involvement
- No active maintenance responsibilities
- May be re-activated if interest returns

## Maintainer Expectations

### Time Commitment

- **Minimum**: 5-10 hours per week on average
- **Peak times**: Up to 20+ hours during release cycles
- **Flexible**: Can adjust with notice based on personal circumstances

### Responsibilities

- **PR Review**: Within 5-7 business days of submission
- **Issues**: Acknowledge within 48 hours; triage and respond regularly
- **Release**: Participate in release planning and execution
- **Community**: Respond to discussions and provide guidance
- **Security**: Rapid response to security reports

## Communication

### Regular Meetings

- **Bi-weekly sync**: Project status and planning (when needed)
- **Ad-hoc**: As needed for urgent decisions

### Decision Making

- Decisions made by consensus when possible
- Documented publicly in issues/PRs
- Rationale communicated to community
- Transparent process even when disagreement exists

## Support and Resources

- **Documentation**: See GOVERNANCE.md and CONTRIBUTING.md
- **Tools**: GitHub, git, container registry access
- **Community**: Access to discussions and feedback
- **Recognition**: Listed as maintainer, credited in releases

## Stepping Down

If a maintainer needs to step down:

1. Notify other maintainers privately
2. Work on transition period (2-4 weeks if possible)
3. Transfer ongoing responsibilities
4. May be recognized as emeritus maintainer
5. Continued recognition for past contributions
