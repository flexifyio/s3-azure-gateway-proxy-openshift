# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We expect contributors to our project to adhere to our Code of Conduct. Whether you're a seasoned open source contributor or a first-timer, treating each other with respect is essential to creating a positive, productive community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being constructive**: Offering helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Being collaborative**: Working together toward common goals
- **Being patient**: Understanding that people have different backgrounds and perspectives
- **Being professional**: Focusing on the work, not personal attacks

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment**: Unwelcome advances, threats, or intimidation
- **Discrimination**: Attacks based on identity, background, or characteristics
- **Disruptive behavior**: Off-topic comments, spam, or deliberate disruption
- **Unethical conduct**: Plagiarism, fraudulent claims, or dishonesty
- **Inappropriate content**: Sexual, violent, or hateful language and imagery

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All spaces within the project (GitHub issues, PRs, discussions, etc.)
- Interactions related to the project on external platforms
- Project-related events and communications
- Represent the project in public spaces

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by:

1. **Directly contacting** project maintainers
2. **Opening a confidential issue** if GitHub provides that option
3. **Emailing** the project maintainers with details

**Please include:**
- Description of the incident
- Who was involved
- When and where it occurred
- Any relevant context or evidence

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### Investigation and Resolution

1. **Report receipt**: You'll receive acknowledgment within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Maintainers will investigate the report
3. **Response**: You'll receive information about the outcome (within reason)
4. **Action**: Appropriate action will be taken based on findings

### Possible Outcomes

Depending on the severity and circumstances, responses may include:

- **Informal resolution**: Direct conversation and clarification
- **Warning**: Formal notice to the individual(s)
- **Temporary suspension**: Removal of participation rights temporarily
- **Permanent ban**: Removal from the community
